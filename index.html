The Sound of Emotion: How Music Shapes Our Feelings

This interactive bar plot allows users to explore the relationship between various music genres and their impact on mental health factors such as Anxiety, Depression, Insomnia, and OCD. The x-axis represents different music genres, while the y-axis shows the average scores for the selected mental health factor. By using the dropdown menu, users can switch between mental health factors, and the chart dynamically updates to reflect the corresponding values. This feature enables users to compare genres and gain insights into how different types of music influence mental health. Simply select a factor from the dropdown and observe the changes in the bar heights to explore the data further.

This line chart highlights trends in the average values of various mental health factors across different age groups.

You can also apply filters to compare groups, such as people who play an instrument or those who compose music.

The Scatterplot visualizes the trends in mental health factors in relation to the number of hours listened to the music.
Out of the 736 participants surveyed, an overwhelming majority of 542 individuals (about 74%) reported that listening to music positively impacted their mental health, highlighting the therapeutic and uplifting role music can play. Meanwhile, 169 participants (approximately 23%) indicated that music had no noticeable effect on their mental well-being, suggesting a more neutral stance. Notably, only 17 participants (around 2%) felt that music had a negative influence on their mental health.
